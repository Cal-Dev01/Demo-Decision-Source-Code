Step-by-Step Skewness Calculation for Age

The skewness of a dataset can be calculated using the formula for skewness, which measures the asymmetry of the data distribution. The formula for skewness (sample skewness) is:

$$ \text{Skewness} = \frac{n}{(n-1)(n-2)} \sum \left( \frac{(X_i - \bar{X})^3}{s^3} \right) $$

Where:

  • \( n \) is the number of observations
  • \( X_i \) is each individual observation
  • \( \bar{X} \) is the mean of the observations
  • \( s \) is the standard deviation of the observations

For the given Age data \([25, 30, 35, 25, 16, 40]\), let's calculate the skewness step-by-step:

Step-by-Step Calculation

  1. Calculate the Mean (\( \bar{X} \)):
    $$ \bar{X} = \frac{25 + 30 + 35 + 25 + 16 + 40}{6} = \frac{171}{6} = 28.5 $$
  2. Calculate the Standard Deviation (s):
    $$ s = \sqrt{\frac{(25 - 28.5)^2 + (30 - 28.5)^2 + (35 - 28.5)^2 + (25 - 28.5)^2 + (16 - 28.5)^2 + (40 - 28.5)^2}{6 - 1}} = \sqrt{\frac{(25 - 28.5)^2 + (30 - 28.5)^2 + (35 - 28.5)^2 + (25 - 28.5)^2 + (16 - 28.5)^2 + (40 - 28.5)^2}{5}} \approx 7.82 $$
  3. Calculate the Skewness:
    $$ \text{Skewness} = \frac{6}{(6-1)(6-2)} \sum \left( \frac{(X_i - 28.5)^3}{(7.82)^3} \right) $$

    Breaking down the individual terms:

    • For \( X_1 = 25 \):
      $$ \left( \frac{25 - 28.5}{7.82} \right)^3 = \left( \frac{-3.5}{7.82} \right)^3 = (-0.4475)^3 = -0.0897 $$
    • For \( X_2 = 30 \):
      $$ \left( \frac{30 - 28.5}{7.82} \right)^3 = \left( \frac{1.5}{7.82} \right)^3 = (0.1918)^3 = 0.0071 $$
    • For \( X_3 = 35 \):
      $$ \left( \frac{35 - 28.5}{7.82} \right)^3 = \left( \frac{6.5}{7.82} \right)^3 = (0.8310)^3 = 0.5748 $$
    • For \( X_4 = 25 \):
      $$ \left( \frac{25 - 28.5}{7.82} \right)^3 = \left( \frac{-3.5}{7.82} \right)^3 = (-0.4475)^3 = -0.0897 $$
    • For \( X_5 = 16 \):
      $$ \left( \frac{16 - 28.5}{7.82} \right)^3 = \left( \frac{-12.5}{7.82} \right)^3 = (-1.5985)^3 = -4.0830 $$
    • For \( X_6 = 40 \):
      $$ \left( \frac{40 - 28.5}{7.82} \right)^3 = \left( \frac{11.5}{7.82} \right)^3 = (1.4716)^3 = 3.1832 $$

    Summing these values:

    $$ \sum \left( \frac{(X_i - 28.5)^3}{(7.82)^3} \right) = -0.0897 + 0.0071 + 0.5748 -0.0897 -4.0830 + 3.1832 = -0.4973 $$

    So, the skewness is:

    $$ \text{Skewness} = \frac{6}{5 \cdot 4} \cdot -0.4973 = -0.1492 $$

    The calculated skewness for the Age data is approximately -0.1492, indicating a slight left-skewed distribution.

Density and Sparsity

Density and sparsity are metrics used to describe the completeness of a dataset, specifically the presence or absence of missing values.

  • Sparsity: The percentage of missing values in the dataset.
    $$ \text{Sparsity} = \left( \frac{\text{Total Missing Values}}{\text{Total Values}} \right) \times 100 $$

    For example, if there are 3 missing values out of 76 total values, the sparsity is calculated as:

    $$ \text{Sparsity} = \left( \frac{3}{76} \right) \times 100 \approx 3.95\% $$

  • Density: The percentage of non-missing values in the dataset.
    $$ \text{Density} = \left( \frac{\text{Total Non-Missing Values}}{\text{Total Values}} \right) \times 100 $$

    For example, if there are 73 non-missing values out of 76 total values, the density is calculated as:

    $$ \text{Density} = \left( \frac{73}{76} \right) \times 100 \approx 96.05\% $$
